# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online: A Student's Prevention Checklist

As Digital Ambassadors, our goal is to keep our campus and our data secure. Here are 8 practical steps you can take right now to protect yourself from common online threats and UPI scams.

### ✅ The Essential Safety Steps

1. **Lock Your Accounts with MFA:** Password-only protection isn't enough anymore. Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on your university email and social media. Using an authenticator app is much safer than getting codes via SMS.
2. **The Golden UPI Rule:** This is the most common scam in India—remember that **you never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money.** If a "buyer" or "sender" asks for your PIN, it’s a trap.
3. **QR Code Red Flags:** Be extremely careful with QR codes sent over WhatsApp or OLX. Scanning a code is an authorization to *send* money out of your account, not to pull money in.
4. **Check the Link Twice:** Before logging into our student portal or any banking site, look closely at the URL. Scammers often use "look-alike" domains (e.g., `unlversity.ac.in` instead of `university.ac.in`).
5. **Ditch the "Universal" Password:** Using the same password for your Netflix, Instagram, and Student ID is a massive risk. Use a password manager to keep unique, complex passwords for every platform.
6. **Avoid "Free" Wi-Fi for Banking:** Open Wi-Fi at cafes or stations is often unencrypted. If you need to check your bank balance or pay a fee, switch to your mobile data or a trusted VPN.
7. **Clean Up Your Social Footprint:** Take five minutes to audit your privacy settings. Your phone number and home address shouldn't be visible to "friends of friends" or the public.
8. **Don't Ignore Updates:** Those annoying "System Update" notifications are actually your best defense. They patch the latest security holes that hackers use to get into your devices.

---

### 🚨 How to Report Cybercrime
If you’ve been targeted or cheated, don't stay silent. Act fast to increase the chances of recovering your funds or securing your identity:

* **Call the National Helpline:** Dial **1930** immediately for any financial or cyber fraud.
* **File an Official Report:** Visit the National Cyber Crime Portal at [**cybercrime.gov.in**](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in).
* **The "Golden Hour":** For UPI or bank fraud, reporting within the first **2 hours** is critical for the bank to freeze the transaction.
